The History of FoodAID Club

Since its inception in 2015, FoodAID Club has been at the forefront of addressing food sustainability issues and making a meaningful impact within the community. From humble beginnings as a student-led initiative, the club has grown into a dynamic organization recognized for its dedication to reducing food wastage, alleviating hunger, and fostering social responsibility.

2015

Founded

FoodAID Club was officially established to raise awareness about food sustainability and support underprivileged families through food distribution programs.

2016

First Major Campaign: "No Food Left Behind"

The club launched its first flagship campaign, collecting surplus food from campus events and local eateries to distribute to those in need.

2018

Expansion of Volunteer Base

With a growing membership, FoodAID Club expanded its outreach programs to include workshops on food sustainability, cooking classes, and community food drives.

2020

Adapting to the Pandemic

Despite challenges posed by COVID-19, the club pivoted to virtual fundraising events and partnered with delivery services to ensure food reached those in need.
